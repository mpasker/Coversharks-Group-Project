How it got started

The game within the game.


CoverSharks was founded by a group of college football fans who have been playing a fantasy sports game called “Pick Six” for over a decade. As an alum and avid fan of the Crimson Tide, Bill Wilder first created the original concept behind the game app in 2006. The idea was simple... give friends and college football fans alike the opportunity to compete in their favorite sport using an innovative format of game play that engages them in a unique, “game within the game” competition and enhances the overall entertainment experience

The CoverSharks Team

Over the past ten years, the players competing in the game have become part of a focus group that has seen vast potential in developing this unique format for play on a national scale. The number of players has steadily grown where the demand now exceeds the limits of the game. The game concept integrated with mobile app technology will make it available to sports fans across the country.

Bill Welder

President and CEO

Bill is a graduate of the University of Alabama with a master's degree in Sport Management and is employed as an administrator with the City of Hoover, Alabama. His years of professional experience in sport management and the fan experience have been a key factor in developing the concept behind the game. Combining app technology with a unique game format has prepared CoverSharks to be on the cutting edge of the mobile sports entertainment industry.

Paul Damron

Chief Financial Officer

Paul currently serves as the Chief Financial Officer for Highlands Regional Medical Center in Sebring, Florida. He has a broad background in finance and accounting with prior experience in public accounting and consulting. Paul holds a bachelor’s degree from Samford University as well as a Master of Business Administration from Vanderbilt University.

Preston Kirk

Chief Marketing Officer

With a bachelor's degree in Broadcast Journalism from the University of Alabama, Preston has worked in sports marketing and management for over ten years. Currently employed with the City of Birmingham, Alabama, he has been involved in marketing and operations at the highest level of collegiate athletics including NCAA National Championships and various conference championship events. Preston has overseen marketing collateral, website, mobile app, and social media design from concept to completion with the idea of enhancing the spectator and athlete experience.
